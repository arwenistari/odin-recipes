’Shroom ’n’ Swiss Burgers

Servings: 2

Total Time: 35 minutes

Prep Time: 10 minutes

Difficulty: Easy

Allergens: Milk, Eggs, Soy, Wheat

Description

If you love mushrooms and burgers, this may be one of your most coveted recipes! Juicy beef patties with saut√©ed mushrooms, honey Dijon, and gooey melty Swiss cheese along with crispy potato wedges perfectly seasoned and on the side makes for a game-changing meal, and all in around 35 minutes in your own home!

Ingredients

  • 4 tsp Olive Oil
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • 1 TBSP Butter (Contains Milk)
  • 12 oz Potatoes
  • 4 oz Button Mushrooms
  • 2 Potato Buns (Contains Milk, Eggs, Soy, Wheat)
  • 1 unit of Mushroom Stock Concentrate
  • 10 oz Ground Beef
  • 2 slices Swiss Cheese (Contains Milk)
  • 3 TBSP Sour Cream (Contains Milk)
  • 1.5 oz Honey Dijon Dressing (Contains Eggs)

Instructions

  1. Adjust rack to top position and preheat oven to 450 degrees. Wash and dry produce. Cut potatoes into 1/2-inch-thick wedges. Trim and thinly slice mushrooms (skip if your mushrooms are pre-sliced!). Halve buns.
  2. Toss potatoes on a baking sheet with a large drizzle of olive oil and a big pinch of salt and pepper. Roast on top rack until browned and crisp, 20-25 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, melt 1 TBSP butter in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add mushrooms and cook, stirring, until lightly browned and softened, 3-5 minutes. Season with salt and pepper. Stir in half the stock concentrate (you'll use the rest in the next step) and 1 TBSP water. Cook, stirring, until mushrooms are coated, 30 seconds. Turn off heat; transfer to a small bowl. Wipe out pan.
  4. In a large bowl, combine beef and remaining stock concentrate. Season with salt and pepper. Form mixture into two patties, each slightly wider than a burger bun. Heat a large drizzle of olive oil in pan used for mushrooms over medium-high heat. Add patties and cook to desired doneness, 3-5 minutes per side. In the last 1-2 minutes of cooking, top each patty with Swiss cheese. Cover pan until cheese melts.
  5. While patties cook, toast buns until golden. In a second bowl, combine sour cream and honey Dijon dressing. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Spread bottom buns with as much sauce as you like. Top with patties, mushrooms, and top buns. Divide burgers and potato wedges between plates. Serve with any remaining sauce on the side for dipping.

    7. Ground Beef is fully cooked when internal temperature reaches 160 degrees.
