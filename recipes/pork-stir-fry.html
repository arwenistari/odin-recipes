Sweet Chili Pork & Cabbage Stir-Fry

Servings: 2

Total Time: 20 minutes

Prep Time: 5 minutes

Difficulty: Easy

Allergens: Sesame, Soy, Wheat, Fish, Milk

Description

Craving a perfect mixture between sweet and savory? This stir-fry bowl is exactly what you need. Featured in this recipe is garlicky ground pork, simmered in a sweet, savory, and spicy blend of soy, ponzu, Thai chili sauce, and rice wine vinegar. The best part? It takes just 20 minutes from start to finish.

Ingredients

  • Cooking Oil
  • 1 TBSP Butter
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • 1/2 tsp Sugar
  • 1/2 cup Jasmine Rice
  • 2 Scallions
  • 4 TBSP Sweet Soy Glaze (Contains Sesame, Soy, Wheat)
  • 1 oz Sweet Thai Chili Sauce
  • 12 ml Ponzu Sauce (Contains Fish, Soy, Wheat)
  • 5 tsp Rice Wine Vinegar
  • 10 oz Ground Pork
  • 1 tsp Garlic Powder
  • 4 oz Red Cabbage & Carrot Mix
  • 1 unit of Crispy Fried Onions (Contains Wheat)

Instructions

  1. Wash and dry produce. In a small pot, combine rice, 3/4 cup water and a big pinch of salt. Bring to a boil, then cover and reduce to a low simmer. Cook until rice is tender, 15-18 minutes. Keep covered off heat until ready to serve.
  2. While rice cooks, trim and thinly slice scallions, separating whites from greens. In a small bowl, combin chili sauce, ponzu, half the vinegar, 2 1/2 TBSP sweet soy glaze, and 1/2 tsp sugar.
  3. Heat a large drizzle of oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add scallion whites and cook until softened, 1 minute. Add pork and garlic powder; season with salt and pepper. Using a spatula, press into an even layer. Cook, undisturbed, until browned on bottom, 3-4 minutes. Break up meat into pieces and continue cooking until pork is cooked through, 1-2 minutes. Stir in sauce and cook, stirring, until slightly thickened, 1 minute more.
  4. Stir red cabbage and carrot mix into pan with pork; cook until just tender, 2-3 minutes. Taste and season with salt and pepper. Fluff rice with a fork; stir in 1 TBSP butter until melted. Divide rice between bowls; top with stir-fry. Garnish with scallion greens and crispy fried onions. Serve.

    5. Ground pork is fully cooked when internal temperature reaches 160 degrees.
